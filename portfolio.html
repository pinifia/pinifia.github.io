Game Music

I have been an active gamer since my childhood, and during my university years, after building a strong network in this field, I started to focus on improving my music skills for games through participating in game jams, alongside freelance work. Later, I collaborated with Tonguç Bodur on some Steam games; Pluviophile and Chionophile, and worked full-time at Zonex Entertainment Studios composing music. I’m still passionate about creating music for games, eager to keep growing in this field, and open to all kinds of exciting projects.

Below, you can find some of my work from various projects.

Let's start with Fenomen Go, the most recent project. It is a game similar to Fall Guys, and I put a lot of effort into its music. I still enjoy listening to these tracks, but the game was never actually released. I want to share them here anyway—although they couldn't be part of a story, maybe more people can enjoy them.

Let's continue with Gladiator's Journey. I composed the entire soundtrack for this game and waited for the team to finish it. Meanwhile, my team was trying to complete several other games and had to prioritize those, so the project was ultimately abandoned before the game could be completed. Still, it was one of the projects I enjoyed the most musically, especially the main theme.

Now let’s talk about Maze Rush. This was a mobile game where you try to solve puzzles by moving stone blocks in an ancient forest. I really loved the first theme I composed for it—it felt like a perfect fit for the game and, in my opinion, was a very strong design choice. Coco was a game we made during an old game jam, and it had amazing visuals. Even though the game is quite old now and its link is no longer available, I had an incredible amount of fun composing its soundtrack.

Jam Simulator was another game jam project and one of the most fun experiences I’ve had while composing game music. We had an amazing team, and I really enjoyed recording the tracks—especially the vocal ones. Still, my favorite part was the cheat music.

Let’s also talk a bit about one of our music albums available on Steam. This story-driven walking simulator was created for people who love rain and nature, and the music album was composed specifically as part of this experience. I’m sharing links to both the game and the album below, and you can also listen to the soundtrack alongside them.

Another game I worked on with Tonguç is Chionophile, a term used in Turkish to describe people who love snow. This album takes a more atmospheric approach and brings the story of a narrative-driven walking simulator to life along the character’s journey. Featuring a wider range of instruments and richer orchestral arrangements, the album reflects the creative freedom of working with a musician-focused producer—something I deeply felt throughout the entire production process. Let’s add a bit of horror as well. The tracks in this playlist are actually demos I composed for Carlos Coronado. After playing Infernium and being deeply impressed by it, I reached out to him and expressed my interest in composing the music for his next game.

Naturally, he asked for demo tracks, and I created these four pieces specifically for that purpose. He really liked them and mentioned that he would contact me if he ever worked on a similar project again—but unfortunately, that game was never made.

Instead, during another game jam, I composed an additional horror track for a game called Dead Whispers, which you can also listen to alongside these demos. Hopefully, in the future, I’ll get the chance to compose music for a full environmental horror game.

Let’s also talk a bit about some digital-focused work. RoboFind was another game jam project of ours, and since it’s a PC game centered around robots, the music had to reflect that mechanical and digital atmosphere. The dark environment became the finishing touch during the composition process and added an extra layer to the overall mood.

PinifiaWorks · RoboFind Soundtracks
PinifiaWorks · Car Crashers Soundtrack

When we think of both digital and retro elements, Car Crashers immediately comes to mind. This PC racing game revolves around cars, where retro themes and vivid colors — and naturally the music — take center stage. It also carries an atmosphere reminiscent of dark, Tron-like visuals, and I tried to merge all of these influences as much as possible in the soundtrack.

Continuing from the darker side, let’s get a bit heavier. This project was a PC shooter game, and recording electric guitars for it was an absolute joy. Since the music aligned perfectly with my rock-loving side and formed a hybrid with digital elements, it still stands among my favorite works — especially the final track =)

PinifiaWorks · Friendly Boy

Finally, Friendly Boy was a dating-themed game we planned to develop for PC. In the game, you roam around the city and build relationships with different girls, and the independence of its tracks comes from the boldness that flirting brings, combined with the innocence created by simply wandering through the city atmosphere.
